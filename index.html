Marex Solutions — Strategist Commentary
17 March 2026 · Day 17 of Iran Conflict · For internal and client distribution

Seventeen days into Operation Epic Fury, energy markets remain severely dislocated. Brent crude at $103/bbl and WTI at $95.20 reflect a $20–25/bbl war premium above pre-conflict fundamentals, with the Strait of Hormuz effectively closed — 21 vessel incidents since 28 February and Gulf oil exports estimated down 60%. Today's first non-Iranian cargo transit with AIS broadcast (the Aframax tanker Karachi) is an encouraging signal, but it remains isolated: Fujairah continues to sustain drone attacks with loadings suspended, and the IRGC's declared closure policy has not been formally rescinded.

The macro implications are increasingly complex. US gasoline at $3.67/gal — up 23% in 14 days — is now the most visible inflation transmission mechanism, and markets have repriced to just one Fed cut in 2026 (December). The 10Y Treasury at 4.23% and DXY near 10-month highs reflect a market grappling with a classic stagflation dynamic: an energy shock simultaneously raising inflation and threatening growth. Gold at $5,016 continues to signal institutional and central bank demand for inflation protection. Wednesday's FOMC decision will be closely watched — the Fed cannot cut without risking an inflation spiral, but it cannot tighten into a slowing economy either.

For structured products, three themes stand out: (1) Capital protection with energy participation — the binary outcome risk argues for structures that participate in further Brent upside while protecting principal in a ceasefire snap-back; (2) Gold volatility structures — elevated implied vol in XAU/USD near $5,000 makes autocallable ELN structures attractive for yield-seeking clients; (3) USD inflation-linked notes — notes linked to US CPI with defined payoffs provide a clean inflation hedge that doesn't require a directional oil view.
